    Tastes

    The exact tastes and taste profiles for beer flavors are largely up for debate among the beer community. However, learning these flavor profiles is critical in building one’s beer proficiency. This page lists a few sites highlighting which tastes and aspects of beer that the general beer community feels are most helpful when describing the many tastes of beer.

    The Splendid Table

    Looking for a great resource to identify not only different beer flavors but also the different styles that have these flavors? Look no further! The Splendid Table identifies seven categories of beer and provides a description of the taste and color of each beer. They also offer notable beer styles for these flavors and even recommended food pairings for each flavor type.

    Visit external site for more information

    Sam Adams

    Sam Adams breaks down beer tasting into five easy steps: appearance, aroma, taste, body, finish. For these, Sam Adams helps those new to beer tasting by giving detailed explanations of what colors, aromas, hops and spices, and textures one should look for in different beers.

    Visit external site for more information

    Dummies

    For those especially new to beer, Dummies gives a pithy, beginner lesson on how to taste beer. Dummies covers three basic tasting traits such as color, bitterness, and gravity, in addition to providing some of the most popular tasting terms.

    Visit external site for more information
